\section{MoA Temporal Reconciliation Evaluation}
\label{sec:three-agent-note}

We evaluate a 4-stage Mixture-of-Agents (MoA) temporal grounding pipeline on a controlled synthetic benchmark with known ground-truth year labels.
The benchmark has 120 samples: 60 explicit, 20 explicit-multi, 20 implicit, and 20 multi-implicit.
\footnote{Explicit: single timestamp target. Explicit-multi: one sentence mentions two entities, and the target is the later entity year. Implicit: one non-event claim with plausible-year uncertainty. Multi-implicit: one sentence combines two implicit claims; target is earliest year where both can hold jointly.}

\paragraph{Pipeline.}
We run three proposer agents (\texttt{gemini-3-flash}, \texttt{claude-4.5-haiku}, \texttt{gpt-5-mini}) and one aggregator (\texttt{gemini-3-flash}) in four stages:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Proposal round 1:} three independent proposer outputs.
  \item \textbf{Aggregation round 1:} aggregator synthesizes round-1 outputs.
  \item \textbf{Proposal round 2:} three proposers re-propose using the round-1 aggregate as auxiliary context.
  \item \textbf{Aggregation round 2:} aggregator synthesizes round-2 outputs.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Scoring.}
For each proposal round, exact-match accuracy is averaged over the three proposer predictions per sample (values in $\{0, \frac13, \frac23, 1\}$), then averaged across samples.
Aggregation rounds are scored as standard binary exact-match on the aggregated year.
For implicit and multi-implicit subsets, we also report Wasserstein distance (Earth Mover distance; lower is better) between predicted year distributions and the ground-truth point mass at the gold year.

\paragraph{Current results (latest run).}
Overall, aggregation round 1 is best (88.3\%), improving over proposal round 1 (85.0\%).
After the second proposer pass, proposal round 2 is 83.1\%, and final aggregation round 2 is 83.3\%.
Performance is near ceiling on explicit variants (96.7--100.0\%), while implicit remains hardest (35.0--46.7\%).
Multi-implicit benefits most from early aggregation (85.0\% at aggregation round 1).
Tables~\ref{tab:overall-stage-accuracy} and~\ref{tab:type-stage-accuracy} provide the exact numerical breakdown behind these statements: the first table reports overall stage-wise exact-match, while the second resolves those same stages by question type.
Tables~\ref{tab:implicit-wasserstein} and~\ref{tab:multi-implicit-wasserstein} complement exact-match with distributional quality, showing where aggregation helps reduce temporal distance even when strict exact-year accuracy is difficult.

\section{Related Work}
Our setup follows layered multi-agent synthesis in MoA pipelines, where parallel first-pass generations are iteratively aggregated and refined \cite{wang2025mixtureofagents}.
For exact-label tasks, self-consistency and voting remain strong controls \cite{wang2022selfconsistency,wang2025ranked}.
The refinement stage is also related to critique/refine and debate-style interaction \cite{madaan2023selfrefine,du2023multiagentdebate}.

\section{Next Steps}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Improve implicit calibration (especially implicit stage-4 regression) via stronger distribution constraints and calibration-aware aggregation.
  \item Compare this fixed MoA schedule against debate-style proposer interactions while keeping the same final aggregation model \cite{du2023multiagentdebate}.
\end{enumerate}
